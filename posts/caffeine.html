A few weeks ago, in the process of cutting back caffeine, I drank a Celsius energy drink (nominally containing 200mg of caffeine) by diluting it with 3 liters of water. This spread out the 200mg over the entire day, and at the end of the day, I felt more wired than I typically do.

To build some intuition around why this would happen, I would like to model the flow of caffeine through my body Specifically, I would like to do so using electronic circuit elements, because those are Well Understood, and doing so makes simulation (using LTSpice) quite straightforward.

modelling assumptions

The biggest assumption we're making is that the speed of caffeine absorption and metabolism is constant over time. Pharmakokinetic research supports this assumption -- the mechanism of caffeine tolerance is in the brain, where the number of adenosine receptors will change.

how does caffeine flow through the body?

  • I consume some liquid that is caffeinated.
  • The liquid collects in my digestive system
  • The caffeine is absorbed into the bloodstream through the stomach and small intestine
  • Caffeine in the bloodstream diffuses (bidirectionally!) through the blood-brain barrier
  • Caffeine is filtered through the kidneys

modelling diffusion

We can model diffusion using an RC circuit

modelling collection

We can model collection of caffeine in a place (e.g stomach) using an integration circuit.
